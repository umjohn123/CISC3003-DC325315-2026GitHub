My Reflection & Growth Journey

Looking back at my academic journey, I recognize significant growth in both technical capabilities and personal development. Each project and course has contributed to shaping my professional identity and approach to problem-solving.

Technical Growth

From Theory to Practice: Initially, I approached programming as an academic exercise. Through projects like the Medical Q&A System, I learned to bridge theory with real‑world application. The challenge wasn't just coding functionality, but designing systems that are reliable, scalable, and user‑focused.

Key Learning: Technical solutions must consider user needs, system constraints, and ethical implications – not just algorithmic efficiency.

Project Management Evolution

From Individual to Collaborative Work: Early projects were largely individual efforts. The Spark‑based Job Market Analysis taught me the value of collaboration, version control best practices, and dividing complex problems into manageable components.

Key Learning: Clear communication, documentation, and regular progress tracking are as important as technical implementation.

Challenges & Solutions

Overcoming Technical Hurdles: Implementing the RAG pipeline for the Medical Q&A system presented significant challenges in data preprocessing and LLM integration. Through systematic debugging, research, and seeking guidance, I developed perseverance and systematic problem‑solving approaches.

Key Learning: Complex problems often require breaking them down, researching existing solutions, and iterative testing.

Personal Development

Tutoring Experience: Teaching "Fundamentals of Big Data Analysis" transformed my understanding of technical concepts. Explaining complex ideas to peers reinforced my own knowledge and developed patience and communication skills.

Key Learning: True mastery comes from being able to teach concepts to others, not just applying them yourself.
